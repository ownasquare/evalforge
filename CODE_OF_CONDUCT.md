# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

EvalForge should be a useful, respectful place to learn and contribute. We welcome people of every
background and experience level.

Helpful participation includes:

- giving specific, constructive feedback;
- assuming good intent while addressing the actual impact of words and actions;
- making space for questions and different levels of familiarity;
- respecting privacy, consent, and the sensitivity of evaluation data; and
- focusing disagreements on ideas, evidence, and user outcomes.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention,
doxxing, sharing private information without permission, deliberate disruption, or retaliation
against someone who reports a concern.

## Scope

This code applies in project repositories, issue trackers, reviews, discussions, events, and other
spaces where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately through the repository host's maintainer-contact or moderation
features when available. Do not open a public issue that identifies a target or includes private
evidence.

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock threads, reject contributions, issue a
warning, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation. Reports should be reviewed as
confidentially and impartially as practical. Good-faith reporters will not be penalized for raising
a concern.
